Northern Vietnam

Hanoi

Hanoi, the capital of Vietnam, is a city where tradition meets modernity. With its rich history, vibrant street life, and mouth-watering cuisine, Hanoi offers a unique travel experience that you can’t miss. From the peaceful beauty of Hoan Kiem Lake and the bustling Old Quarter to the elegance of French colonial architecture, every corner of Hanoi tells a story. Visitors can savor the world-famous pho on the street, sip strong Vietnamese coffee at a roadside café, or explore centuries-old temples nestled among modern buildings. Whether you're a history lover, a foodie, or an adventurer, Hanoi has something special waiting for you.

1. Hoan Kiem Lake

Hoan Kiem Lake, also known as the Lake of the Returned Sword, is a scenic spot in the heart of Hanoi. It is surrounded by lush greenery and is a popular place for locals and tourists to relax, jog, or enjoy a leisurely stroll. The lake is home to the iconic Turtle Tower and Ngoc Son Temple, which can be reached by a charming red bridge. The area is especially beautiful at sunrise and sunset when the lake reflects the vibrant colors of the sky.

2. The Old Quarter

The Old Quarter is a bustling neighborhood known for its narrow streets, colonial architecture, and vibrant street markets. Each street is named after the goods that were once sold there, such as Hang Bac (Silver Street) and Hang Gai (Silk Street). Visitors can explore local shops, sample street food, and experience the lively atmosphere of this historic area. Do not forget to try the famous egg coffee at one of the many cafés!

3. Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum

The Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum is a significant landmark in Hanoi, dedicated to the founder of modern Vietnam. The mausoleum houses the preserved body of Ho Chi Minh, and visitors can pay their respects while learning about his life and contributions to the country. The surrounding Ba Dinh Square is also worth a visit, especially during the changing of the guard ceremony.

4. The Temple of Literature

The Temple of Literature is Vietnam's first national university and a beautiful example of traditional Vietnamese architecture. The complex features serene gardens, ancient trees, and stunning courtyards. It is a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city and a great place to learn about Vietnam is educational history.

Ha Long Bay

Ha Long Bay, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famous for its emerald waters and thousands of towering limestone islands topped with rainforests. A cruise on the bay is a must-do activity, allowing visitors to explore hidden caves, floating fishing villages, and enjoy fresh seafood. The bay is also home to diverse wildlife and offers opportunities for kayaking, swimming, and hiking. Whether you choose to relax on a cruise or explore the islands, Ha Long Bay is a breathtaking destination that showcases Vietnam's natural beauty.

1. Sung Sot Cave (Surprise Cave)

Sung Sot Cave is one of the largest and most impressive caves in Ha Long Bay. It features two main chambers filled with stunning stalactites and stalagmites. The cave's unique formations and breathtaking views make it a must-visit attraction for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

2. Ti Top Island

Ti Top Island is known for its white sandy beach and excellent panoramic view of Ha Long Bay. A short hike to the top offers breathtaking photo opportunities. It’s a great place to swim, sunbathe, or relax after a cruise.

3. Bai Tu Long Bay

Located northeast of Ha Long Bay, Bai Tu Long is quieter and less crowded. It offers crystal-clear water, untouched beaches, and stunning limestone formations. Ideal for travelers who prefer a peaceful, off-the-beaten-path experience.

4. Luon Cave

Luon Cave is only accessible by kayak or bamboo boat, leading you into a peaceful lagoon surrounded by towering cliffs. It’s a serene place to explore nature up close. You may spot monkeys, birds, and exotic plants as you glide along the calm water.

Sapa

Sapa is a picturesque mountain town located in Lao Cai Province, near the Chinese border. Known for its cool climate and misty peaks, Sapa is surrounded by majestic mountains and endless rice terraces. The region is home to various ethnic minority groups such as the Hmong, Dao, and Tay, each with rich traditions and colorful clothing. Visitors can trek through scenic valleys, explore local villages, and enjoy cultural homestays. Sapa is perfect for nature lovers, hikers, and anyone seeking peaceful landscapes and authentic cultural experiences.

1. Fansipan Mountain

Nicknamed “The Roof of Indochina,” Fansipan is the highest mountain in Vietnam at 3,143 meters. You can take a scenic cable car ride or challenge yourself with a rewarding hike. The views from the top are absolutely stunning and well worth the trip. On clear days, you’ll feel like you're floating above the clouds. The area around the summit is also decorated with Buddhist statues and temples, adding a spiritual touch to the journey.

2. Cat Cat Village

Ti Top Island is known for its white sandy beach and excellent panoramic view of Ha Long Bay. A short hike to the top offers breathtaking photo opportunities. It’s a great place to swim, sunbathe, or relax after a cruise. Visitors can watch local artisans weaving and dyeing fabrics using age-old techniques. It’s also a great spot to photograph terraced fields and traditional stilt houses.

3. Muong Hoa Valley

Muong Hoa Valley is home to breathtaking terraced rice fields and ethnic minority communities. It’s a top destination for trekking and cultural immersion. The natural beauty and warmth of the local people make it unforgettable. Ancient rock carvings found in the valley add a mysterious and historical element to the landscape. During harvest season, the golden rice fields create postcard-perfect scenery.

Ha Giang

Sapa is a picturesque mountain town located in Lao Cai Province, near the Chinese border. Known for its cool climate and misty peaks, Sapa is surrounded by majestic mountains and endless rice terraces. The region is home to various ethnic minority groups such as the Hmong, Dao, and Tay, each with rich traditions and colorful clothing. Visitors can trek through scenic valleys, explore local villages, and enjoy cultural homestays. Sapa is perfect for nature lovers, hikers, and anyone seeking peaceful landscapes and authentic cultural experiences.

1. Dong Van Karst Plateau

The Dong Van Karst Plateau is a UNESCO Global Geopark known for its dramatic limestone landscapes. It’s a place of geological wonder, with deep valleys and ancient rock formations. Traditional ethnic villages dot the area, offering rich cultural experiences.

2. Ma Pi Leng Pass

Ma Pi Leng is considered one of the most spectacular mountain passes in Vietnam. It winds along cliffs high above the Nho Que River, offering jaw-dropping views. Riding or driving along this pass is an unforgettable adventure.

3. Lung Cu Flag Tower

Lung Cu marks the northernmost point of Vietnam, standing proudly on Dragon Mountain. The flag tower symbolizes national pride and offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and even into China. It’s a meaningful stop for travelers exploring Ha Giang.

3. Quan Ba Heaven’s Gate

Quan Ba Heaven’s Gate is a scenic viewpoint where you can see the unique Twin Mountains (Fairy Bosom Hills). The misty valley views at sunrise or sunset are breathtaking. It’s the perfect place to pause, reflect, and take in the majestic scenery.

Central Vietnam

Da Nang – Hoi An

Da Nang – Hoi An is a must-visit duo in Central Vietnam, offering the best of both modern city life and timeless heritage. Da Nang impresses visitors with its long sandy beaches like My Khe, iconic sights such as the Marble Mountains and Golden Bridge, and a vibrant yet relaxed atmosphere. Just a short drive away lies the enchanting ancient town of Hoi An, a UNESCO World Heritage Site filled with lantern-lit streets, yellow colonial buildings, and traditional markets. Together, these two destinations offer everything from nature, culture, and history to delicious cuisine — from mi Quang in Da Nang to cao lầu in Hoi An. You can relax at An Bang Beach, stroll through the lantern festival, or enjoy street food by the river. Whether you're seeking adventure, romance, or cultural immersion, Da Nang – Hoi An promises a rich and unforgettable travel experience.

1. Golden Bridge (Cầu Vàng) – Ba Na Hills, Da Nang

This iconic pedestrian bridge is held by two giant stone hands and floats above the clouds at Ba Na Hills. The surreal design and panoramic mountain views make it one of the most photographed landmarks in Vietnam. The surrounding area features a European-style village, gardens, and a cable car ride that’s one of the longest in the world.

2. My Khe Beach – Da Nang

My Khe is a long, clean beach known for its soft white sand and calm waves. It’s ideal for swimming, surfing, or simply relaxing under the sun. With plenty of restaurants and cafes nearby, it’s a popular spot for both locals and tourists.

3. Marble Mountains (Ngũ Hành Sơn) – Da Nang

A group of five limestone and marble hills, each named after a natural element. The mountains are home to pagodas, caves, and viewpoints that offer sweeping views of the coastline. Climbing the stone steps and exploring hidden Buddhist sanctuaries is a must-do experience.

4. Hoi An Ancient Town

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hoi An is known for its lantern-lit streets, yellow colonial houses, and riverside charm. Walking through the Old Town feels like stepping back in time. You can enjoy custom tailoring, street food, and monthly lantern festivals.

Hue

Hue is the former imperial capital of Vietnam and a city full of historical significance. Located along the Perfume River, Hue is home to the ancient Imperial City (Citadel), royal tombs, pagodas, and palaces from the Nguyen Dynasty. Visitors can take a dragon boat ride, explore the peaceful Thien Mu Pagoda, and admire the stunning architecture of tombs like Khai Dinh and Tu Duc. Hue’s cuisine is considered some of the finest in Vietnam, known for dishes like bún bò Huế and colorful royal-style meals. Unlike the hustle of other cities, Hue has a slower, elegant pace and deep cultural vibe. The city is also a great place to learn about Vietnamese history and traditions. It’s ideal for travelers interested in heritage and relaxation.

1. Imperial City (Citadel) of Hue

The Imperial City was the former capital of the Nguyen Dynasty and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Inside its ancient walls are palaces, temples, and royal tombs rich with history. Visitors can spend hours wandering through its courtyards, pavilions, and gates.

2. Thien Mu Pagoda

Perched on the banks of the Perfume River, Thien Mu Pagoda is one of the oldest and most iconic religious sites in Vietnam. Its seven-tiered tower offers fantastic photo opportunities. The tranquil setting is ideal for a peaceful walk or meditation.

3. Tomb of Khai Dinh

Unlike other imperial tombs, Khai Dinh’s tomb is a striking blend of Vietnamese and European architecture. The ornate interiors, mosaic tiles, and mountain views make it stand out. Climbing the grand staircase to the tomb offers an unforgettable view.

4. Perfume River Cruise

Taking a dragon boat cruise on the Perfume River is a relaxing way to explore Hue. You’ll pass by pagodas, rural villages, and historical landmarks. Sunset cruises are especially scenic and romantic.

Phong Nha – Ke Bang

Located in Quang Binh Province, Phong Nha – Ke Bang is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its spectacular cave systems. It is home to Son Doong Cave, the largest cave in the world, as well as other famous caves like Paradise Cave and Phong Nha Cave. Adventurers can trek through jungle trails, swim in underground rivers, and camp inside giant caverns. The surrounding countryside offers rice paddies, mountains, and friendly rural villages. Eco-tourism is thriving here, making it perfect for nature lovers and explorers. Despite its global fame, Phong Nha still retains a quiet and authentic charm. It’s a must-see for those seeking off-the-beaten-path experiences in Vietnam.

1. Phong Nha Cave

This is one of the most accessible and beautiful caves in the national park. You’ll enter the cave by boat along the Son River and discover dramatic rock formations, underground rivers, and peaceful silence. It’s a great introduction to the region’s karst landscape.

2. Paradise Cave (Thiên Đường Cave)

As its name suggests, this cave looks like a fairy tale with thousands of sparkling stalactites and stalagmites. The wooden walkway inside makes it easy for visitors to explore. It’s larger and more dramatic than Phong Nha Cave, yet still easily accessible.

Da Lat

Da Lat, located in the Central Highlands of Vietnam, is a peaceful mountain city famous for its cool climate, romantic atmosphere, and flower-covered landscapes. Often called the "City of Eternal Spring", Da Lat offers year-round mild weather, making it a favorite escape from the tropical heat of other regions. The city is surrounded by pine forests, lakes, waterfalls, and colorful flower gardens that feel like a European town in the heart of Vietnam. Highlights include Xuan Huong Lake, Dalat Flower Garden, and the quirky Crazy House. It’s also a great place to enjoy fresh produce, local coffee, and homemade jams. Whether you're taking a swan boat on the lake or exploring French colonial villas, Da Lat offers a peaceful, romantic getaway. It’s especially popular for couples, photographers, and travelers seeking nature and relaxation.

1. Xuan Huong Lake

Located in the heart of Da Lat, this crescent-shaped lake is ideal for walking, cycling, or riding a swan boat. The flower-lined paths and cool breeze create a romantic and relaxing vibe. It’s also beautiful at night when the lights reflect on the water.

2. Dalat Flower Garden

Da Lat is known as the “City of Flowers,” and the Flower Garden showcases its colorful reputation. You’ll find over 300 types of flowers arranged into beautiful displays. It’s a perfect place for photography and a peaceful stroll.

3. Crazy House (Hằng Nga Guesthouse)

This bizarre yet fascinating building looks like something out of a dream. With twisting staircases, tree-like walls, and hidden rooms, it’s both a piece of architecture and an art installation. Visitors can explore freely or even book a night’s stay!

4. Datanla Waterfalls

Just a few kilometers from town, Datanla offers a mix of nature and fun. You can walk to the falls or take a thrilling alpine coaster ride through the forest. The waterfall itself is beautiful and surrounded by greenery.

Southern Vietnam

Ho Chi Minh City (Saigon)

Ho Chi Minh City, formerly known as Saigon, is Vietnam’s largest and most energetic metropolis. It’s a vibrant mix of modern skyscrapers, historic French colonial buildings, and lively street life. The city offers everything from rooftop bars and luxury malls to chaotic markets and delicious street food. Landmarks like the War Remnants Museum, Notre-Dame Cathedral, and Ben Thanh Market reflect the city’s rich and complex history. Ho Chi Minh City never really sleeps — it’s always buzzing with activity, making it a great place for those who enjoy fast-paced urban life. Coffee shops, motorbikes, and friendly locals define the city’s rhythm. It's a gateway to the south and a must-visit for first-time travelers to Vietnam.

1. War Remnants Museum

This museum offers a powerful look into the Vietnam War through photos, documents, and military artifacts. It’s one of the most visited attractions in the city, helping visitors understand Vietnam’s modern history. The exhibitions can be emotional, but they provide an honest and educational experience. It’s a must-visit for those interested in the country’s past and resilience.

2. Notre-Dame Cathedral of Saigon

Built by the French in the late 19th century, this iconic cathedral stands in the heart of District 1. With its red brick walls and twin bell towers, it’s a beautiful example of colonial architecture. The surrounding area is a lively square with street vendors, coffee carts, and photo opportunities. Though the interior may be under restoration, the exterior remains a major city landmark.

3. Ben Thanh Market

Ben Thanh is a bustling traditional market filled with souvenirs, clothes, handicrafts, and street food. It’s a great place to shop and practice bargaining skills while soaking in the energy of local trade. Try Vietnamese snacks like bánh xèo or fresh spring rolls while you browse.

4. Nguyen Hue Walking Street

Nguyen Hue is a pedestrian boulevard lined with cafés, shops, and fountains, perfect for evening strolls. Locals and tourists gather here for events, photos, and street performances. It’s especially magical during festivals when the area is decorated with lights and flowers.

5. Landmark 81

Landmark 81 is the tallest building in Vietnam and a modern symbol of Ho Chi Minh City. Located in Vinhomes Central Park, it features a luxury mall, SkyView observatory, fine dining, and a 5-star hotel. The panoramic view from the 79th floor and stunning night lights make it a must-visit.

Mekong Delta

The Mekong Delta is a lush, tropical region in southern Vietnam where life flows with the rhythm of the rivers. Known as Vietnam’s “rice bowl,” it features endless rice paddies, fruit orchards, and floating markets. Visitors can cruise through narrow canals, visit traditional villages, and sample fresh tropical fruits. The floating markets like Cai Rang in Can Tho give a glimpse into the unique river-based culture of the region. It’s also home to many ethnic communities and a slower, peaceful way of life. From sunrise boat rides to homestays by the water, the Mekong Delta offers a truly authentic cultural experience. Nature, food, and hospitality are at the heart of this region.

1. Cai Rang Floating Market (Can Tho)

This is the largest and most famous floating market in the Mekong Delta. Boats gather early in the morning to trade fruits, vegetables, and daily goods directly on the river. It’s a unique and colorful scene, best experienced by boat tour. Don’t forget to try fresh pineapple or a bowl of noodles from a floating vendor!

2. Tra Su Cajuput Forest (An Giang)

Tra Su is a beautiful flooded forest filled with cajuput trees and green duckweed-covered water. Visitors glide through the forest by boat, enjoying the peaceful sounds of birds and nature. It’s also a sanctuary for many bird species.

3. Vinh Trang Pagoda (My Tho)

This ornate Buddhist pagoda blends Vietnamese, Chinese, and European architectural styles. It features large statues, peaceful gardens, and intricate carvings. The serene atmosphere makes it a lovely stop during your Delta journey. It’s one of the oldest and most respected religious sites in the region.

Phu Quoc Island

Located in the Gulf of Thailand, Phu Quoc is Vietnam’s largest island and a tropical paradise. With its white sandy beaches, turquoise waters, and luxury resorts, it’s a favorite destination for relaxation and island adventures. You can explore pristine beaches like Sao Beach and Long Beach, visit the colorful Dinh Cau Night Market, or take a cable car ride over the sea. The island also has nature reserves, pepper farms, and local fishing villages to explore. Phu Quoc is perfect for swimming, snorkeling, diving, and catching stunning sunsets. It offers a great mix of natural beauty and modern comforts. Whether you want a romantic escape or a family beach holiday, Phu Quoc has it all.

1. Sao Beach (Bãi Sao)

Famous for its powdery white sand and turquoise waters, Sao Beach is one of the most beautiful on the island. The waves are gentle, making it ideal for swimming or simply relaxing. Beachside cafes serve fresh seafood and coconut water. It’s a perfect tropical escape away from the crowds.

2. Sunset Sanato Beach Club

Located on the west coast, this beach club is known for its artistic seafront installations and beautiful sunset views. It’s a trendy spot to enjoy cocktails, music, and beachside vibes. The large swings and metal statues make for perfect Instagram photos. It’s popular among younger travelers and couples.

3. Grand World Phu Quoc

Grand World is a vibrant entertainment and shopping complex known as the “Sleepless City” of Phu Quoc. It features colorful European-style architecture, night markets, museums, and exciting shows that run day and night. You can take a gondola ride along the Venice-inspired canal or enjoy street performances and cultural parades.

4. Hon Thom Cable Car (Pineapple Island)

The world’s longest oversea cable car ride offers breathtaking views of the sea and surrounding islets. It takes you to Hon Thom Island, where you can enjoy water activities and a beach resort. The ride itself is scenic and thrilling. It’s a fun and scenic adventure for all ages.

Vung Tau

Just a two-hour drive from Ho Chi Minh City, Vung Tau is a popular beach getaway for locals and tourists alike. It features sandy beaches like Back Beach and Front Beach, where visitors can swim, relax, or enjoy fresh seafood. The city is also home to the famous Christ the King Statue, which offers panoramic views from the top. You can stroll along the seaside promenade, explore local temples, or hike up to White Palace. Vung Tau offers a more laid-back atmosphere compared to the busy streets of Saigon. It’s a great weekend escape for those who enjoy sea breeze and coastal scenery.

1. Back Beach (Bãi Sau)

Back Beach is Vung Tau’s most popular beach with a long sandy shoreline and good waves. It’s great for swimming, sunbathing, or renting a beach umbrella for a relaxing day. The promenade is lined with cafes and restaurants. Early morning and sunset are the best times to visit.

2. Christ the King Statue (Tượng Chúa Giêsu)

This towering statue stands atop Nui Nho hill and is one of the tallest of its kind in Asia. Visitors can climb hundreds of steps for panoramic views of the ocean and city. You can even go inside and up to the statue’s arms. It’s both a spiritual and scenic highlight.

3. White Palace (Bạch Dinh)

A colonial-era villa built by the French, the White Palace overlooks the coast and has a rich history. It once hosted emperors and governors, and now serves as a museum. Surrounded by trees and breezy views, it’s a peaceful stop for history lovers. The architecture and coastal view are stunning.
