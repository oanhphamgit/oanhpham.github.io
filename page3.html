Transportation Tips

  • Use ride-hailing apps like Grab for taxis and motorbike rides. It's safer, cheaper, and avoids language barriers compared to hailing taxis on the street.
  • Negotiate fares in advance if you take a regular taxi or cyclo (three-wheeled bicycle taxi), especially in touristy areas.
  • Motorbike rentals are common, but always check the bike carefully before renting and wear a helmet at all times (it's required by law).
  • Traffic can be chaotic, especially in big cities like Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City. Always walk carefully across the street â€” walk slowly and steadily so drivers can predict your movement.
  • Public buses are cheap but may be confusing if you don't speak Vietnamese. Use apps like BusMap or ask locals for help.
  • Domestic flights are very affordable if you want to travel long distances quickly (e.g., from Hanoi to Da Nang or Ho Chi Minh City).
  • Trains in Vietnam offer a scenic way to travel, especially the routes from Hanoi to Da Nang (Hai Van Pass area is beautiful).
  • Be cautious with overnight buses (also called "sleeper buses") â€” choose reputable companies for better safety and comfort.
